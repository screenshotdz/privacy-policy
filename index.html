Privacy Policy

Smart Files Manager

This Privacy Policy explains how Smart Files Manager collects, uses, stores, and protects information related to your use of the application.

Effective Date: [05-06-2026] Last Updated: [01-07-2026]

1. Introduction

Smart Files Manager (“the App”) is an Android application that helps users browse, organize, manage, compress, extract, analyze, protect, and share files and folders stored on their devices. This Privacy Policy is designed to be clear, transparent, and suitable for use inside the application and on a public website.

We respect your privacy and aim to process your data in a way that is necessary for the core features of the App. In general, the App processes files locally on your device whenever possible and does not upload personal files to our servers without your explicit action.

2. Information We Collect

Personal Information

The App does not generally require users to create an account or provide personal identity information in order to use its core file management features. If you contact us for support, you may choose to provide personal information such as your name, email address, or other contact details.

Non-Personal Information

We may collect non-personal information such as app version, operating system version, language preferences, feature usage patterns, and diagnostic information that helps us improve performance and stability.

Device Information

We may collect limited device-related information, including device model, Android version, storage information, and technical identifiers necessary to provide compatibility and support features.

Usage Information

The App may collect information about how you use its functions, such as file browsing, compression, extraction, folder management, search activity, and storage analysis. This information is generally used to improve user experience and application reliability.

Crash Reports

If the App crashes or encounters errors, diagnostic reports may be collected to help identify and fix issues. These reports may include technical information such as error messages, stack traces, device model, and operating system details.

Analytics (if enabled)

If analytics features are enabled, the App may collect aggregated usage information such as feature interaction statistics and app performance metrics. Analytics data is typically anonymized or pseudonymized where possible and may be used to understand feature adoption and improve the App.

3. Permissions Used

The App requests certain Android permissions to provide its features. These permissions are used only as needed and are not requested for unrelated purposes.

  • Storage: Used to browse, read, copy, move, rename, delete, compress, extract, and analyze files stored on internal storage, SD cards, and external storage devices.
  • Photos and Videos: Used when the App needs to display, organize, or share media files stored on your device.
  • Notifications: Used if the App provides background alerts, completion notices, or other user-facing notifications.
  • Biometric Authentication: Used to protect hidden folders, encrypted files, or sensitive content with fingerprint or face recognition where supported by the device.
  • Camera: Used only if a feature such as scanning documents, capturing images, or creating media is offered by the App. If such functionality is not enabled, the Camera permission is not used.
  • Internet: Used for optional cloud synchronization, backup, or file transfer features when you choose to connect to cloud services.
  • USB Access: Used when the App interacts with USB OTG storage devices or other external storage connected to the device.

4. How Information Is Used

We use information collected by the App only for legitimate purposes related to the operation, improvement, and support of the App. These purposes include:

  • Managing and organizing files and folders.
  • Performing compression and extraction of archives.
  • Providing backup, synchronization, and cloud-related features when enabled by the user.
  • Improving application stability, performance, and compatibility.
  • Providing support and troubleshooting services.
  • Enforcing security and privacy protections such as biometric access and encryption.

5. Local Processing

The App is designed to process files locally on your device whenever possible. File scanning, browsing, compression, extraction, and storage analysis are generally performed locally and do not require uploading personal files to external servers.

The App does not upload personal files to our systems without your explicit action. If you choose to use optional cloud services, your data may be transferred to the selected provider according to your instructions and their own privacy terms.

6. Cloud Services

The App may support optional integrations with cloud storage platforms such as Google Drive, OneDrive, Dropbox, or similar services. These integrations are optional and are only used when you choose to connect your account or initiate a transfer.

When you use cloud services, your data may be processed by the respective cloud provider in accordance with their privacy policies. Please review the privacy terms of those providers directly before using such features.

7. File Security

The App may provide features that help protect your data, including encryption of selected files, biometric protection for hidden folders or secure content, secure deletion options, and local storage protections. These features are intended to improve privacy and reduce unauthorized access on the device.

However, no method of storage or transmission is completely secure. You are responsible for keeping your device secure and safeguarding your credentials, recovery information, and access methods.

8. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. The App does not share your personal information with third parties for advertising or commercial resale purposes.

Your information may be shared only in limited circumstances, such as when you explicitly choose to share files using Android sharing features, or when disclosure is required by law, legal process, or governmental request.

9. Third-Party Services

The App may use third-party services such as Firebase Crashlytics, Firebase Analytics, Google Play Services, or similar platforms to improve reliability and diagnostics. These services may collect technical and usage data in accordance with their own privacy policies.

We encourage you to review the privacy policies of these providers before using related features. The App does not control the privacy practices of third-party services.

10. Children’s Privacy

The App is not intended for children under the applicable age in your jurisdiction. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children without appropriate parental consent. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us so that we can delete it promptly.

11. User Rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Request deletion of your personal information.
  • Export or obtain a copy of certain information.
  • Disable optional analytics or telemetry features.
  • Revoke permissions previously granted to the App.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided below. Some rights may be limited where applicable law permits.

12. Data Retention

We retain information only for as long as necessary to provide the App’s features, support users, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Locally stored information may remain on your device until you delete it, uninstall the App, or clear relevant app data.

Diagnostic and analytics data may be retained by us or by our service providers according to their retention policies.

13. Security Measures

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information against unauthorized access, misuse, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures may include encryption, secure storage practices, permission-based access control, and adherence to Android security best practices.

Despite these efforts, users should remain aware that no digital service can guarantee absolute security.
